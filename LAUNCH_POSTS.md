# VoiceThing Feedback Posts

Use these as starting points. The best feedback will come from tailoring the
same idea to each audience instead of dropping identical posts everywhere.

## Reddit: creator communities

Suggested subreddits: `r/podcasting`, `r/VoiceActing`, `r/VoiceOver`,
`r/NewTubers`, `r/youtubers`, `r/Twitch`, `r/streaming`.

Title:

```text
Would you want alerts if your voice showed up in videos or podcasts you did not authorize?
```

Post:

```text
My friend and I are exploring an idea called VoiceThing: identity monitoring for your voice.

The rough version:
- You provide a verified sample of your own voice
- The tool monitors public audio/video sources
- It flags possible appearances of your real voice or an AI clone
- You review the source, timestamp, confidence level, and context
- If it looks like misuse, it helps prepare evidence or takedown material

We are not selling anything yet. We are trying to understand whether this is a real problem for creators, podcasters, voice actors, streamers, journalists, or anyone whose voice is public online.

A few questions:
1. Is unauthorized voice cloning or reuse something you actually worry about?
2. Which platforms would matter most to monitor first?
3. Would you trust a tool that required a voice sample?
4. What privacy controls would be non-negotiable?
5. Would alerts alone be useful, or would takedown/evidence support matter more?

Blunt feedback is welcome.
```

## Reddit: privacy/security communities

Suggested subreddits: `r/privacy`, `r/cybersecurity`, `r/artificial`.

Title:

```text
Would you trust a voice-misuse monitoring tool if it required your voice sample?
```

Post:

```text
My friend and I are validating a product idea: a web app that alerts people when their voice, or a likely AI clone of it, appears in public online audio/video.

The trust problem is obvious: to protect someone's voice, the product may need a reference sample of that same voice.

We are thinking through safeguards like:
- explicit consent for every uploaded sample
- encryption
- deletion controls
- short retention windows
- human review before treating any match as misuse
- clear evidence exports instead of automatic accusations

I would love critical feedback from privacy/security-minded people:
1. Would you ever use something like this?
2. What would immediately make it unacceptable?
3. Is local processing necessary, or would encrypted cloud processing be acceptable?
4. What should the product never do?
5. What would a trustworthy first version look like?
```

## Founder/product communities

Suggested platforms: Indie Hackers, Hacker News `Ask HN`, `r/SideProject`,
`r/SaaS`, `r/indiehackers`, `r/EntrepreneurRideAlong`.

Title:

```text
Validating VoiceThing: identity monitoring for your voice
```

Post:

```text
My friend and I are validating VoiceThing, a landing-page-stage idea for voice identity monitoring.

Problem:
AI voice cloning is getting easier, but most people only find out about misuse after someone else sees or hears it.

Possible product:
Creators, podcasters, voice actors, streamers, journalists, and public-facing teams upload verified voice samples. The product monitors public audio/video sources and alerts them when it finds possible unauthorized use of their real voice or an AI clone.

We think the wedge may be narrow at first:
Voice misuse monitoring for creators on YouTube, podcasts, and short-form video.

Big risks:
- trust barrier around uploading voice samples
- false positives
- platform coverage
- unclear willingness to pay
- whether alerts are enough without takedown support

What would you test first? Who is the sharpest first customer? Would you position this as creator protection, biometric privacy, brand monitoring, or something else?
```
